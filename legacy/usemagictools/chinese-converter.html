Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between Simplified and Traditional Chinese?
Simplified Chinese was introduced in mainland China in the 1950s with fewer strokes for easier writing. Traditional Chinese preserves the historical evolution of characters and is widely used in Hong Kong, Macau, Taiwan, and overseas Chinese communities.
Why distinguish between Hong Kong Traditional and Taiwan Traditional?
While both use Traditional characters, some vocabulary differs. For example, 'software' is '軟件' in Hong Kong but '軟體' in Taiwan; 'taxi' is '的士' in Hong Kong but '計程車' in Taiwan. This tool intelligently handles these regional differences.
Will the formatting be preserved after conversion?
Yes, the original line breaks and spacing are preserved. This tool only converts Chinese characters without changing the text layout, ensuring the converted content maintains its original formatting.
Is there a character limit?
Theoretically unlimited, but very long texts may affect browser performance. It's recommended to keep single conversions under 100,000 characters. For larger texts, process in batches.
What conversion engine is used?
This tool uses the core algorithm from OpenCC (Open Chinese Convert), one of the most accurate Chinese conversion engines, capable of handling complex cases like one-to-many character mappings and regional vocabulary differences.
Is this tool really free? Is it safe?
Yes, this tool is 100% free with no ads, no signup, no usage limits, and no watermarks. All processing happens in your browser — your data never leaves your device. It's completely safe and private.
